About Me

I am a passionate AI researcher pursuing my PhD at ENET'Com (National School of Electronics and Telecommunications) in Computer Engineering. My work sits at the intersection of Large Language Models, information retrieval, and autonomous systems.

My doctoral research focuses on addressing key limitations in current LLM systems, particularly around factual accuracy, reasoning capabilities, and real-time processing of streaming data. I explore how retrieval-augmented approaches can enhance LLM knowledge bases while maintaining computational efficiency.

I'm particularly interested in the emerging field of agentic AI, where LLMs are empowered with planning, memory, and tool-use capabilities to solve complex, multi-step problems autonomously. My work aims to bridge the gap between static LLM knowledge and dynamic, real-world information systems.

Research Focus

Large Language Models

Investigating architectural improvements, efficiency optimizations, and novel training methodologies for transformer-based models with a focus on knowledge retention and reasoning capabilities.

RAG Systems

Developing advanced Retrieval-Augmented Generation frameworks that dynamically integrate external knowledge sources with LLMs to improve factual accuracy and reduce hallucination.

Agentic AI

Building autonomous agent systems with planning, memory, and tool-use capabilities that can solve complex, multi-step problems through reasoning and interaction with external environments.
